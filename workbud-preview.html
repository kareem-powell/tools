What is The Den?
The Den is your daily home base. It surfaces the two things you should be focused on most right now as large, clickable cards at the top — your primary focus (linked directly to the relevant tool) and your secondary one. Below that, a three-cell snapshot shows the metrics that matter for your role. At the bottom, all your key links live in one place so you're never hunting across tabs. When you set up your own Work Bud, Claude configures The Den based on your actual role and priorities.

What is Deliverables?
Deliverables tracks everything you're working on in one place, organized into three buckets: what you're actively in the middle of right now, what's queued up and waiting, and what you've already finished. Tasks pull from Asana by default, but can be wired to any project management tool. Each morning Work Bud scans your projects and updates this tab automatically — you see where you stand the moment you open it. You can check things off as you go, and the progress bar at the top updates in real time.

What is Meeting Notes?
Meeting Notes is a running log of notes from your recurring meetings, auto-organized by date and meeting type. Each morning Work Bud generates a fresh entry for meetings happening that day, pre-populated with the relevant context pulled from your calendar and recent project activity. After each meeting you can add your own notes directly here — and they'll be included in the context for the next briefing. Click any card to expand it.

What is Call-Outs?
Call-Outs is a running log of genuine recognition moments — Slack messages, DMs, comments from teammates, or anything else that captures real feedback on your work. Claude scans your connected tools periodically to surface these automatically and adds them here with the source and context. The goal is simple: so you always have a clear record of what you've contributed and how your work has landed, without having to remember to save it yourself.

What is Lovable Builds?
Lovable Builds is your toolbox and change log for Work Bud itself. It lists the tools and platforms used to build and run everything here — and importantly, it includes a Lovable integration that lets you make changes to the site using plain English without ever touching code or opening GitHub. You describe what you want changed, Lovable builds it, and it deploys automatically. The build ideas section at the bottom is a running wishlist of things that could be added next.
