Function · FP&A
Sales Finance

Sales Finance owns deal economics, revenue integrity, and the financial planning layer on top of Amplitude's commercial motion. It partners with Sales on deal structure, sets the analytical framework for how bookings translate to revenue, and ensures the numbers that hit the books are accurate — quarter over quarter.

High Output Management · Output Definition
What does Sales Finance produce?

In HOM terms, the output of Sales Finance is reliable financial intelligence that enables better decisions. That means accurate planning models, validated deal economics, clean revenue recognition, and the proactive surfacing of risk before it becomes a problem. FP&A's job is to make the invisible legible — translating complex deal activity into numbers leadership can act on.

Function · RevRec & Compliance
Accounting

Accounting is the final downstream recipient of every deal OM processes. It owns revenue recognition, ensures bookings comply with ASC 606, and closes the loop between what Sales promised and what Finance can recognize. When OM delivers a clean booking, Accounting moves fast. When it doesn't, Accounting creates the case that sends it back.

High Output Management · Output Definition
What does Accounting produce?

Accounting's output is recognized revenue — the moment a deal moves from committed ARR to booked revenue on Amplitude's financial statements. In MWM terms, the key result is accuracy: every deal that hits the books reflects the true economic agreement. The secondary output is speed: the faster Accounting closes, the faster Finance can report and leadership can decide.
