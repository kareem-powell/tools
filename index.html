Order Management

OM sits at the center of Amplitude's commercial motion — downstream of Sales and upstream of Finance and Accounting. It validates that every closed deal is correctly structured before revenue is recognized. When OM catches an error first, the company closes faster and cleaner. When it misses one, Accounting inherits the problem.

How to set up your own Work Bud with Cowork expand ↓

Work Bud is built on plain HTML deployed to GitHub Pages. To adapt it for your own OM workflow, paste the following initialization prompt into a Cowork session:

You are setting up a personal Work Bud ops hub.

Files to use as a base:
  workbud.html — personal ops center (password-protect with your chosen password)
  bookings.html — OM deal validation checklist
  index.html — House of O.M. department dashboard

Connect the following:
  - Asana: your project GID (find it in your project URL)
  - Salesforce: your org's SFDC URL + Case Queue view
  - Slack: channels relevant to your OM/Finance workflow
  - Google Calendar: for morning briefing scheduling

Set up these Cowork scheduled skills:
  - Morning briefing (Slack digest + tasks + calendar + schedule)
  - EOD recap (completed vs open task summary → Slack DM)
  - ATR progress tracker (weekly ATR case count update)
  - Meeting notes generator (creates note stubs from calendar)

Update workbud.html with your name, Asana project GID,
Salesforce URL, and Slack user ID. Set your password.

FP&A · Sales Finance
Sales Finance

Sales Finance (FP&A) owns the financial planning layer on top of Amplitude's commercial motion — deal economics, ARR forecasting, forward-looking risk monitoring, and revenue integrity. It sets the assumptions that Accounting closes against and the models that leadership decides from. FP&A sits above both Accounting and OM in the data flow: plan first, track second, report third.

High Output Management · Output Definition
What does Sales Finance produce?

Reliable financial intelligence that enables faster, better decisions. That means accurate ARR forecasts, validated deal economics, proactive overage alerts before accounts breach contract limits, and clean ATR data before it creates downstream RevRec problems. FP&A makes the invisible legible — turning complex deal activity into numbers leadership can act on.

Vol Ramp ATR Cleanup — 138 Deals
A "Vol Ramp" deal lets a customer grow into a contract over time — but the ATR (Authorization to Recognize revenue) value on 138 of these deals was calculated using the wrong baseline (average ARR instead of exit-year ACV, or annual contract value). Sales Finance owns the correction. Each fixed deal unblocks Accounting from finalizing that revenue.
HIGH · Sales Finance owns · ~60% complete
AI Week — G&A Consumption Monitoring Agent
An AI tool being built to predict which accounts are trending toward exceeding their contracted usage limits — months before it happens. When an account uses more of the product than their contract covers (a "G&A overage"), it creates billing disputes and RevRec complications. This agent surfaces those risks early so the right teams can act first.

Function · RevRec & Close
Accounting

Accounting owns the close — ARR reconciliation, revenue recognition against ASC 606, NetSuite integration, and the monthly sync between what Sales booked and what Finance can report. It is the final checkpoint in Amplitude's revenue flow. Every OM booking that's wrong creates rework here; every clean booking accelerates close. Accounting closes against the assumptions FP&A set and the bookings OM validated.

High Output Management · Output Definition
What does Accounting produce?

Recognized revenue — the moment a committed deal moves onto Amplitude's financial statements accurately and on time. In MWM terms: accuracy (every booked deal reflects the true economic agreement) and speed (close completes on schedule so Finance can report and leadership can decide). Accounting's leverage point is upstream quality — the cleaner OM's bookings, the faster Accounting closes.

ARR Reconciliation & Post-Close Verification
Each month, Accounting reconciles the company's annual recurring revenue (ARR) ledger — verifying that every deal's ARR, overages, and discounts are recorded correctly in NetSuite (the finance system) against what Salesforce shows. Any gap means a correction before leadership sees the numbers.
RECURRING · Monthly close cycle

ATR Case Resolution — Upstream Dependency
ATR (Authorization to Recognize revenue) is a trigger that must be validated before a deal can be officially counted as revenue. Open ATR cases in the OM queue each represent a deal where that validation is still pending — and each one holds up a corresponding entry in Accounting. Sales Finance and OM own the fix; Accounting tracks the downstream impact.

Sales Finance · FP&A
Financial Planning & Analysis

Financial Planning & Analysis owns the forward-looking layer of Sales Finance — ARR forecasting models, Vol Ramp assumptions, consumption monitoring, and the planning cycle that sets targets for the rest of the org. FP&A is where deals become projections and projections become decisions.

Planning Cycle · How FP&A Works
Plan → Forecast → Align → Report

FP&A runs on a quarterly cadence anchored by the close. Each month: actuals come in from Accounting, get loaded into Anaplan, and variance analysis begins. Each quarter: ARR forecast is certified with CEO approval, department narratives go out, and a new estimate is set for the following period. H1 is the current planning window.
